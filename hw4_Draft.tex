\textbf{Problem 1.}

Suppose $X$ and $Y$ have joint pdf $f(x,y) = c(x^2 + xy)$ on $[0,1] \times [0,1]$.

(a) Find $c$ and the joint cdf $F(x,y)$.

Setting $\int_0^1 \int_0^1 c(x^2 + xy) \, dx \, dy = 1$:
$$c \int_0^1 \int_0^1 (x^2 + xy) \, dx \, dy = c \cdot \frac{7}{12} = 1$$
Therefore $c = \frac{12}{7}$.

$$F(x,y) = \int_0^y \int_0^x \frac{12}{7}(u^2 + uv) \, du \, dv = \frac{x^2y(4x + 3y)}{7}$$

(b) Find the marginal cumulative distribution functions $F_X$ and $F_Y$ and the marginal pdf $f_X$ and $f_Y$.

$$f_X(x) = \int_0^1 \frac{12}{7}(x^2 + xy) \, dy = \frac{6x(2x + 1)}{7}$$
$$f_Y(y) = \int_0^1 \frac{12}{7}(x^2 + xy) \, dx = \frac{4 + 6y}{7}$$
$$F_X(x) = \frac{2x^2(4x + 3)}{7}, \quad F_Y(y) = \frac{y(4 + 3y)}{7}$$

(c) Find $E[X]$ and $\text{Var}(X)$.

$$E[X] = \int_0^1 x \cdot \frac{6x(2x + 1)}{7} \, dx = \frac{5}{7}$$
$$E[X^2] = \int_0^1 x^2 \cdot \frac{6x(2x + 1)}{7} \, dx = \frac{39}{70}$$
$$\text{Var}(X) = \frac{39}{70} - \left(\frac{5}{7}\right)^2 = \frac{23}{490}$$

(d) Find the covariance and correlation of $X$ and $Y$.

$$E[Y] = \frac{4}{7}, \quad E[XY] = \frac{17}{42}$$
$$\text{Cov}(X,Y) = \frac{17}{42} - \frac{5}{7} \cdot \frac{4}{7} = -\frac{1}{294}$$
$$\text{Var}(Y) = \frac{23}{294}, \quad \text{Cor}(X,Y) = -\frac{\sqrt{15}}{69}$$

(e) Are $X$ and $Y$ independent?

No. Since $\text{Cov}(X,Y) \neq 0$, $X$ and $Y$ are not independent.

\textbf{Problem 2. Independence}

Marginal pmfs: $P(X=i) = P(Y=j) = \frac{1}{3}$ for $i,j \in \{1,2,3\}$.

For independence, we need $P(X=i,Y=j) = \frac{1}{9}$ for all $i,j$.
However, $P(X=1,Y=1) = \frac{1}{18} \neq \frac{1}{9}$.

Therefore, $X$ and $Y$ are not independent.

\textbf{Problem 3. Correlation}

(a) Joint distribution:
\begin{align}
P(X=1,Y=1) &= c\\
P(X=1,Y=-1) &= \frac{1}{2} - c\\
P(X=-1,Y=1) &= \frac{1}{2} - c\\
P(X=-1,Y=-1) &= c
\end{align}

$E[X] = E[Y] = 0$, $E[XY] = 4c - 1$

$\text{Cov}(X,Y) = 4c - 1$, $\text{Cor}(X,Y) = 4c - 1$

(b) Independence: $\text{Cov}(X,Y) = 0 \Rightarrow c = \frac{1}{4}$

100\% correlation: $|4c - 1| = 1 \Rightarrow c = 0$ or $c = \frac{1}{2}$

\textbf{Problem 5. Overlapping sums}

$X = X_1 + \cdots + X_n$, $Y = X_{n-7} + \cdots + X_{2n-8}$

Overlap: $X_{n-7}, \ldots, X_n$ (8 terms)

$\text{Cov}(X,Y) = 8 \times \text{Var}(X_i) = 8 \times \frac{1}{4} = 2$

$\text{Var}(X) = \text{Var}(Y) = \frac{n}{4}$

$\text{Cor}(X,Y) = \frac{2}{\sqrt{\frac{n}{4} \cdot \frac{n}{4}}} = \frac{8}{n}$
